Festivals of India

Diwali

November 1, 2026

The festival of lights celebrating the victory of light over darkness. Homes are illuminated with diyas and fireworks light up the sky across the nation.

Holi

March 20, 2026

The vibrant festival of colors that marks the arrival of spring. People gather to play with colors, dance, and enjoy festive foods together.

Eid-ul-Fitr

March 21, 2026

Celebrated by the Muslim community, Eid marks the end of Ramadan. It is a time for prayers, feasts, and sharing gifts with loved ones.

Christmas

December 25, 2026

Celebrated with great enthusiasm, especially in India. Churches are decorated, carols are sung, and families gather for festive meals.

Pongal

January 14-17, 2026

A harvest festival from Tamil Nadu celebrating gratitude to the Sun God. Traditional dishes like Pongal are prepared and shared.

Baisakhi

April 13-14, 2026

Celebrated in Punjab, Baisakhi marks the Sikh New Year and the harvest season. It is a time of joy, dance, and thanksgiving.

Navratri

October 17-26, 2026

Nine nights of worship of Goddess Durga across India. Garba and Dandiya Raas are performed in Gujarat and West Bengal.

Onam

August 27 - September 8, 2026

Kerala's biggest harvest festival with boat races, flower carpets, and traditional feasts celebrating King Mahabali.

Eid-ul-Adha

June 7, 2026

Festival of sacrifice commemorating Ibrahim's willingness to sacrifice his son. Celebrated with prayers and sharing of meat.

Mahashivratri

February 15, 2026

Night of Lord Shiva's marriage. Devotees fast, chant Om Namah Shivaya, and visit Shiva temples across India.

Janmashtami

September 3, 2026

Birthday of Lord Krishna. Dahi Handi celebrations recreate Krishna's love for butter. Popular in Maharashtra and North India.

Guru Purnima

July 12, 2026

Day to honor teachers and spiritual gurus. Students express gratitude to their teachers who illuminate their lives.

Classical Dance Forms of India

Bharatanatyam

Tamil Nadu

One of the oldest classical dance forms, known for its purity of line, clarity of expression, and intricate footwork. Originated in temple dance tradition.

Kathak

North India

A classical dance known for its intricate footwork, spins, and storytelling through expressions and gestures. Developed in Mughal courts.

Odissi

Odisha

Known for its fluid movements, sculpturesque poses, and devotion to Lord Jagannath. One of the oldest dance forms dating back to 2nd century BC.

Kuchipudi

Andhra Pradesh

A dance-drama that combines graceful dance movements with dramatic acting and martial arts elements. Known for dancing on brass plate.

Mohiniyattam

Kerala

The Dance of the Enchantress - known for its gentle, graceful movements and Lasya (grace) element. Performed by women.

Kathakali

Kerala

A highly stylized classical Indian dance-drama known for its elaborate costumes, makeup (pacha), and dramatic performances based on Ramayana.

Manipuri

Manipur

Known for its gentle, graceful movements and circular choreographies. The famous Ras Lila depicts Lord Krishna's dance with gopis.

Sattriya

Assam

Founded by Srimanta Sankaradeva in 15th century, this dance is performed in Sattra monasteries. Known for rhythmic movements and devotional theme.

Traditional Arts & Crafts

Madhubani

Origin: Bihar
Ancient folk art characterized by bold colors, geometric patterns, and depictions of nature and mythology.

Warli Art

Origin: Maharashtra
Simple yet expressive art using basic geometric shapes to depict daily life and nature.

Kalamkari

Origin: Andhra Pradesh
Hand-painted or block-printed cotton fabric depicting mythological stories and Hindu deities.

Bandhani

Origin: Gujarat & Rajasthan
Tie-dye technique creating beautiful patterns on fabric, especially on sarees and dupattas.

Blue Pottery

Origin: Jaipur
Distinctive turquoise blue glazed pottery with floral and geometric patterns.

Pattachitra

Origin: Odisha
Scroll painting depicting Hindu mythological stories with bold lines and vivid colors.
